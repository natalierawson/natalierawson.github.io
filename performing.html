Bio

Natalie graduated from The Boston Conservatory at Berklee in 2014 with a BM in Vocal Performance. During her time at school, she performed scenes from Le Nozze di Figaro (Marcellina) and The Medium (Mrs. Gobineau), as well as being cast in the school's production of Die Zauberflote. She also attended the Orfeo Music Festival in Vipiteno, Italy. Here she she performed scenes from Carmen (Mercedes) and La Rondine (Lisette).

Since graduating, Natalie has been performing solo work around southern Massachusetts. She was cast as Violet in Plymouth Community Theater's 2014 production of "A Christmas House". She has has also had a steady run singing with First Parish Church in Duxbury, serving both as a section leader for the senior choir and a featured soloist for important services throughout the year.

Starting in 2015, Natalie has been an active member of The Tanglewood Festival Chorus. As well as yearly runs with The Boston Pops to perform their Holiday Pops programs under the direction of Keith Lockhart, Natalie has greatly enjoyed joining the chorus in a wide variety of repertoire during the main season. One of her favorite performances was when she traveled with the TFC to Carnegie Hall to perform Prokofiev's Alexander Nevsky. She has also performed A Midsummer Night's Dream (Mendelssohn), "Dixi" (Kancheli), On the Transmigration of Souls (Adams), Reqiuem in D K.626 (Mozart), Symphony No. 2 Op. 14 "October" (Shostakovich), and Daphnis et Chloé (Ravel) with the chorus.

Up until very recently, Natalie was based in Massachusetts. However, Natalie is now settled in the UK, in Birmingham. In her spare time she is continuing to work on solo repertoire for opera, musical theater, and jazz. She is also hoping to branch into voice over work. She is excited to see what the future holds for her performance career!
